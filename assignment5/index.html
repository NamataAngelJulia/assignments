EDUCATION IN UGANDA

What is education?

Education is the process of facilitating learning, or the acquisition of skills, values, morals, beliefs and habits. Education can take place in a formao or informal setting and any experience that has a formative effect on the way one thinks, feels or acts can be considered educational.

It is sometimes referred to as the key to success.

There are four stages of formal education ie:

  • nursery
  • primary
  • secondary
  • higher education

nursery education

During this stage, a child learns the basics such as spelling, writing, reading, aswell as some basic life skills like interacting with others. There are 3 classes in this stage ie; baby class, middle class, and top class and it is for chldren aged 3-5 years.

primary education

This is where a child some useful skills aswell as learn about their environment and bodies. there are four major subjects in this stage and a few minor ones and the stage has 7 classes. it is for children aged 5-14 years (ideally). To advance from primary one must pass the Primary Leaving Examinations.

secondary

during this stage, students study in depths about various things in different subjects .
This stage has 2 levels ie; O level and A level and graduating form each level requires one to pass UCE and UACE examinations respectively. It is at this stage where one discovers what they want to do as a career and hence major in that in higher education institutes.

higher education

This includes University and Tertiary institutes.
Here one majors in a certain field and studies it in order to practice that as a career. This stage is completed by Graduation. From here, one should be ready for the working environment.
